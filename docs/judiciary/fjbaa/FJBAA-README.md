## Overview

FJBAA addresses fundamental dysfunctions in the federal judiciary: circuit boundaries reflecting nineteenth-century geography rather than contemporary population, arbitrary appointment opportunities based on actuarial chance, national anxiety over justice health and longevity, concentration of power in a single Chief Justice for decades, and eroded confirmation norms. The Act replaces norm-dependent processes with structural mechanisms that function regardless of partisan conditions.
