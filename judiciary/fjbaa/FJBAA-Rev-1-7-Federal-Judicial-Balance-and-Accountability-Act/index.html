    FEDERAL JUDICIAL BALANCE AND ACCOUNTABILITY ACT¶

    A Bill to Modernize the Federal Judiciary, Establish Population-Based Judicial Circuits, Align Supreme Court Composition with Circuit Organization, and Ensure Balanced Representation¶

    Revision 1.7¶

    SECTION 1: SHORT TITLE¶

    This Act may be cited as the "Federal Judicial Balance and Accountability Act."

    SECTION 2: FINDINGS AND PURPOSES¶

    (a) Findings¶

    Congress finds that:

    1. Article III of the Constitution vests judicial power in "one supreme Court, and in such inferior Courts as the Congress may from time to time ordain and establish";

    2. The Constitution provides that judges "shall hold their Offices during good Behaviour" and receive compensation that "shall not be diminished during their Continuance in Office";

    3. Congress has constitutional authority under Article III and the Necessary and Proper Clause to organize and structure the federal judiciary to ensure efficient administration of justice;

    4. The current federal circuit system reflects historical boundaries rather than population distribution, creating unequal representation and workload disparities;

    5. The Supreme Court has maintained nine justices since 1869, despite the creation of additional federal circuits and dramatic population growth;

    6. The constitutional tradition of Supreme Court justices serving as circuit justices creates a direct representational relationship between regional circuits and the Supreme Court, with optimal judicial administration achieved when each regional circuit is served by one Supreme Court justice;

    7. Regular and predictable judicial appointments promote constitutional balance and reduce partisan manipulation of the appointment process;

    8. Clear definition of judicial duties and responsibilities promotes effective court administration and public confidence in the judiciary;

    9. The Senate's "Advice and Consent" power is most effectively exercised when nominees possess robust and transparent records of legal reasoning available for public evaluation;

    10. The current system creates arbitrary advantages for presidents based on actuarial chance rather than constitutional design;

    11. The position of Chief Justice should rotate among justices as a capstone to distinguished service rather than concentrating administrative power in a single individual for decades;

    12. A structured career path for Supreme Court justices -- progressing from Associate Justice to Chief Justice to Senior Justice -- provides clarity, dignity, and predictability while preserving life tenure under Article III;

    13. Public confidence in the federal judiciary requires transparent and enforceable standards of ethical conduct applicable to all federal judges, including Justices of the Supreme Court;

    14. Epistemic diversity -- incorporating the perspectives of career judges, legal scholars, experienced legislators, executive branch legal officers, and practitioners -- strengthens the Court's capacity to interpret law within a complex democratic society.

    (b) Purposes¶

    The purposes of this Act are to:

    1. Establish population-based federal judicial circuits to ensure equal representation;

    2. Align Supreme Court composition with regional circuit organization to restore constitutional balance between Court size and judicial administration;

    3. Create predictable and fair judicial appointment processes;

    4. Define the duties and responsibilities of federal judicial offices;

    5. Establish transparency and documentation requirements that ensure nominees possess evaluable records of legal reasoning;

    6. Enhance transparency in the judicial appointment process;

    7. Ensure optimal utilization of judicial experience and expertise;

    8. Restore constitutional balance among the branches of government;

    9. Diffuse the concentration of power in the Chief Justice position through automatic rotation;

    10. Establish a dignified three-phase career structure for Supreme Court justices that removes the nation's constitutional future from dependence on individual justices' health and longevity;

    11. Establish binding ethical standards and accountability mechanisms for the Supreme Court that preserve judicial independence while ensuring that no Justice is above the rule of law.

    SECTION 3: DEFINITIONS¶

    In this Act:

    1. "Associate Justice" means a justice of the Supreme Court serving in the first ten years of active service, performing the regular duties of hearing and deciding cases before the Court;

    2. "Chief Justice" means the justice of the Supreme Court serving in their eleventh and twelfth years of active service, who presides over the Court and serves as administrative head of the federal judiciary;

    3. "Senior Justice" means a justice of the Supreme Court who has completed twelve years of active service and transitioned to senior status, retaining full Article III protections and life tenure while serving in a reduced-duty capacity;

    4. "Active service" means service as an Associate Justice or Chief Justice, comprising the first twelve years of a justice's tenure on the Supreme Court;

    5. "Federal judge" means any judge appointed under Article III of the Constitution;

    6. "Population-based circuit" means a judicial circuit organized primarily according to population distribution rather than state boundaries;

    7. "Regional circuit" means one of the fifteen numbered judicial circuits serving approximately equal populations across the United States;

    8. "Ethics Review Panel" means the body of five Senior Justices established under Section 503 to adjudicate ethics complaints against Justices serving in active service;

    9. "Judicial Inspector General" means the independent officer established under Section 504 to receive complaints and conduct investigations into alleged ethical violations;

    10. "Bridge Panel" means retired Chief Judges of United States Courts of Appeals who serve on the Ethics Review Panel during the transition period as provided in Section 508;

    11. "Qualifying documentation" means works of substantial legal analysis that satisfy the requirements of Section 302;

    12. "Substantial legal analysis" means written work demonstrating sustained engagement with legal questions through reasoned argument, interpretation of legal authorities, and application of legal principles to factual circumstances.

    TITLE I: FEDERAL CIRCUIT REORGANIZATION¶

    Section 101. Establishment of Fifteen Judicial Circuits¶

    (a) Circuit Structure¶

    Beginning January 1, 2027, the United States shall be divided into fifteen judicial circuits for the administration of federal justice, organized as follows:

    1. Fifteen Regional Circuits numbered First through Fifteenth, each serving approximately equal populations as determined by the most recent decennial census;

    2. District of Columbia Circuit retaining specialized jurisdiction over federal regulatory, administrative, and governmental matters;

    3. Federal Circuit retaining specialized jurisdiction over patents, international trade, federal employment, and appeals from specialized federal courts.

    (b) Population-Based Boundaries¶

    The Administrative Office of the United States Courts shall, within 180 days of enactment:

    1. Divide the national population (excluding territories) into fifteen approximately equal segments;

    2. Assign each segment to a numbered circuit, prioritizing geographic contiguity where feasible;

    3. Ensure no single circuit serves more than 110% or less than 90% of the average circuit population.

    (c) Decennial Rebalancing¶

    Following each decennial census, the Administrative Office shall:

    1. Recalculate optimal circuit boundaries to maintain population equality;

    2. Propose boundary adjustments to Congress;

    3. Implement approved adjustments effective January 1 following the census.

    (d) Specialized Circuits Unchanged¶

    The District of Columbia Circuit and Federal Circuit shall retain their current jurisdictions and organizational structures.

    Section 102. Judicial Appointments to New Circuits¶

    (a) Circuit Judges¶

    Within two years of circuit reorganization:

    1. The President shall nominate, and the Senate shall consider, sufficient circuit judges to maintain adequate judicial resources in each circuit;

    2. Each regional circuit shall have no fewer than six active judges;

    3. Existing circuit judges shall be reassigned according to the jurisdiction where they maintain primary residence.

    (b) District Judges¶

    District court structure and judgeships shall continue unchanged, except that:

    1. District courts shall be assigned to new circuit jurisdictions based on geographic location;

    2. Appeals from district courts shall proceed to the appropriate regional circuit;

    3. Congress may create additional district judgeships as needed.

    TITLE II: SUPREME COURT COMPOSITION AND APPOINTMENTS¶

    Section 201. Supreme Court Composition¶

    (a) Court Structure¶

    The Supreme Court of the United States shall consist of fifteen justice positions, with each justice progressing through the three-phase career structure established by this Act:

    1. Active service comprises the first twelve years of each justice's tenure and includes two phases:
    2. (A) Associate Justice (years one through ten), performing the regular duties of hearing and deciding cases before the Court; and

    3. (B) Chief Justice (years eleven and twelve), presiding over the Court and serving as administrative head of the federal judiciary;

    4. Senior Justice status begins upon completion of active service, with justices retaining full Article III protections and life tenure while serving in a reduced-duty capacity.

    At steady state, the Court shall have fifteen justices in active service: fourteen in the Associate Justice phase and one in the Chief Justice phase.

    (b) Alignment with Circuits¶

    The Supreme Court composition reflects:

    1. Fifteen regional judicial circuits, each corresponding to one justice position;

    2. Restoration of the constitutional relationship between circuit organization and Supreme Court size, whereby each regional circuit is served by one circuit justice;

    3. The Chief Justice, in addition to their assigned regional circuit, provides circuit justice coverage for the District of Columbia Circuit and the Federal Circuit.

    (c) Phased Expansion Schedule¶

    The Court shall expand gradually according to the following timeline:

    1. Initial Expansion (Years 1-6): One additional justice appointed in each odd-numbered year until all fifteen justice positions are filled;

    2. Regular Replacement (Year 13+): Beginning twelve years after the first appointment under this Act, one justice completes active service and transitions to Senior Justice each odd-numbered year;

    3. Steady State: Once fully implemented, each presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities.

    Section 202. Appointment Process¶

    (a) Presidential Nomination¶

    The President shall nominate individuals to fill Associate Justice vacancies within 90 days of a vacancy occurring or being scheduled.

    (b) Senate Consideration¶

    The Senate shall:

    1. Hold confirmation hearings within 60 days of receiving a nomination;

    2. Vote on confirmation within 120 days of receiving a nomination;

    3. Provide a floor vote unless the nominee withdraws or the President rescinds the nomination.

    (c) Predictable Appointment Schedule¶

    Beginning twelve years after the first appointment under Section 201(c):

    1. One justice position becomes available each odd-numbered year;

    2. The most senior justice appointed under this Act's expansion schedule transitions to Senior Justice;

    3. Each four-year presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities.

    Section 203. Three-Phase Justice Career Structure¶

    (a) Associate Justice (Years 1-10)¶

    Upon appointment and confirmation to the Supreme Court, a justice serves as Associate Justice for ten years, during which they:

    1. Hear and decide cases before the Supreme Court;

    2. Participate fully in oral arguments, conferences, and deliberations;

    3. Author opinions assigned by the Chief Justice or senior Associate Justice in the majority;

    4. Serve as circuit justice for an assigned regional circuit.

    (b) Chief Justice (Years 11-12)¶

    Upon commencing their eleventh year of active service, the most senior Associate Justice who has not previously served as Chief Justice automatically becomes Chief Justice for two years, during which they:

    1. Preside over Court sessions and conferences;

    2. Assign majority opinions when in the majority;

    3. Serve as administrative head of the federal judiciary;

    4. Preside over presidential impeachment trials in the Senate;

    5. Exercise no greater voting power than Associate Justices.

    (c) Senior Justice (Year 13+)¶

    Upon completing twelve years of active service, a justice transitions to Senior Justice, where they:

    1. Retain full Article III status and constitutional protections;

    2. Continue to receive full judicial salary and benefits;

    3. Maintain life tenure during "good behavior";

    4. May hear and decide cases as assigned by the Chief Justice;

    5. May sit by designation on federal courts of appeals;

    6. May serve on judicial panels, commissions, and committees;

    7. May decline assignments without forfeiting judicial status or compensation;

    8. May be called upon to serve on the Ethics Review Panel established under Title V.

    (d) Voluntary Early Transition¶

    Any Associate Justice may elect to transition directly to Senior Justice before completing ten years of active service, forgoing service as Chief Justice and creating an immediate vacancy for presidential appointment.

    Section 204. Chief Justice Provisions¶

    (a) Automatic Selection¶

    Beginning when the first justice appointed under Section 201(c) reaches their eleventh year of active service:

    1. The Associate Justice with the longest tenure in active service who has not previously served as Chief Justice shall become Chief Justice upon commencing their eleventh year of active service;

    2. No separate nomination or confirmation shall be required;

    3. The transition to Chief Justice shall occur automatically on the anniversary of the justice's appointment to active service.

    (b) Transition Period¶

    Until the automatic selection process begins under subsection (a):

    1. The Chief Justice serving on the effective date of this Act shall continue in that capacity until the automatic selection process begins under subsection (a), death, voluntary election of Senior Justice status, or removal. Upon commencement of the automatic selection process, the Chief Justice serving under this paragraph shall revert to the status of Associate Justice and may continue in active service or elect Senior Justice status under Section 403(b);

    2. If a vacancy in the Chief Justice position occurs before the automatic selection process begins, the President shall designate a Chief Justice from among the sitting Associate Justices, subject to Senate confirmation, to serve until the automatic selection process begins.

    (c) Incapacity or Vacancy¶

    1. If the Chief Justice is temporarily unable to perform duties, the Associate Justice with the longest tenure in active service shall serve as Acting Chief Justice;

    2. If the Chief Justice dies, is removed, or elects early transition to Senior Justice during their term, the Associate Justice next in line under the automatic selection process shall become Chief Justice for the remainder of their own eleventh and twelfth years of active service;

    3. If no Associate Justice has reached their eleventh year of active service when a vacancy occurs, subsection (b)(2) shall apply.

    TITLE III: TRANSPARENCY AND EVALUATIVE STANDARDS FOR NOMINATIONS¶

    Section 301. Congressional Findings¶

    Congress finds that:

    1. The "Advice and Consent" power of the Senate is most effectively exercised when a nominee possesses a robust and transparent record of legal reasoning available for public evaluation;

    2. Epistemic diversity -- incorporating the perspectives of career judges, legal scholars, experienced legislators, and executive branch legal officers -- strengthens the Court's capacity to interpret law within a complex democratic society;

    3. Requiring documented evidence of sustained legal reasoning ensures that a nominee's judicial philosophy is grounded in a demonstrated history of analysis rather than undisclosed commitments or confirmation hearing performance;

    4. Nothing in this Title shall be construed to limit the President's authority under Article II to nominate any person of the President's choosing to the Supreme Court.

    Section 302. Nomination Documentation Requirements¶

    (a) Required Submission¶

    The President shall submit to the Senate, concurrently with any nomination to the Supreme Court of the United States, a comprehensive record demonstrating the nominee's legal reasoning and constitutional philosophy. This record shall consist of no fewer than fifty (50) distinct works of substantial legal analysis authored or primarily drafted by the nominee.

    (b) Qualifying Documentation¶

    The submission required under subsection (a) may include any combination of the following:

    1. Judicial Works: Opinions, orders, concurrences, or dissents -- published or unpublished -- from any federal or state court of record;

    2. Scholarly Works: Legal scholarship published in peer-reviewed journals, law reviews, edited volumes, or academic treatises, and scholarly books on legal subjects;

    3. Advocacy Works: Legal briefs, substantive motions, or petitions filed in federal appellate courts, the Supreme Court of the United States, or the highest court of any State;

    4. Legislative Works: Formal committee reports containing legal analysis, legislative findings of a legal nature, or legal memoranda prepared during service in a federal or state legislature;

    5. Executive Works: Official legal opinions or memoranda prepared during service in a federal or state executive capacity, including service as Attorney General, Solicitor General, or in agency general counsel offices;

    6. Professional Works: Formal legal opinions or memoranda prepared in the course of professional legal practice that demonstrate sustained engagement with complex constitutional, statutory, or regulatory questions.

    (c) Subject Matter Diversity¶

    The submitted record shall include works addressing at least three of the following subject areas:

    1. Constitutional structure, federalism, or separation of powers;

    2. Individual rights under the Bill of Rights or Fourteenth Amendment;

    3. Federal jurisdiction, justiciability, or procedure;

    4. Statutory interpretation or legislative process;

    5. Administrative law, agency authority, or regulatory interpretation;

    6. Criminal law, procedure, or sentencing.

    (d) Attestation of Authorship¶

    The nominee shall provide a signed statement certifying that they were the primary author or principal drafter of each work submitted. For collaborative works, the nominee shall describe their specific contribution to the legal analysis contained therein.

    Section 303. Public Disclosure and Evaluation Period¶

    (a) Public Availability¶

    The Senate Judiciary Committee shall make all documentation submitted under Section 302 available to the public in a searchable electronic format within seventy-two (72) hours of receipt.

    (b) Minimum Deliberation Period¶

    The Senate shall not hold a final vote on the confirmation of any Supreme Court nominee until at least forty-five (45) days have elapsed from the date the documentation required under Section 302 is made publicly available.

    (c) Supplementation¶

    The Senate Judiciary Committee may request supplemental materials if it determines that the initial submission does not adequately permit evaluation of the nominee's legal reasoning in a subject area specified in Section 302(c). Such request shall identify the deficiency and the categories of additional materials sought.

    Section 304. Nominee Statements¶

    (a) Required Statements¶

    Each Supreme Court nominee shall provide:

    1. Professional Biography: A detailed account of the nominee's legal career, including all legal employment, representative matters or areas of practice, and professional achievements;

    2. Judicial Philosophy Statement: A written statement of no fewer than 5,000 words addressing:

    3. (A) The nominee's approach to constitutional interpretation;
    4. (B) The nominee's view of the role of precedent in constitutional and statutory cases;
    5. (C) The nominee's understanding of the judicial function within the constitutional structure;

    6. (D) At least three illustrative examples from the nominee's submitted documentation demonstrating application of the principles described;

    7. Financial Disclosure: Complete financial disclosure as required by the Ethics in Government Act of 1978, as amended.

    (b) Public Disclosure¶

    All statements required under subsection (a) shall be made publicly available concurrent with the documentation required under Section 302.

    Section 305. Senate Consideration¶

    (a) Completeness Determination¶

    Within fourteen (14) days of receiving a nomination, the Senate Judiciary Committee shall determine whether the documentation submitted satisfies the requirements of Sections 302 and 304. If the Committee determines the submission incomplete, it shall notify the President of the deficiency.

    (b) Consideration Timeline¶

    The Senate shall complete its consideration of a Supreme Court nomination within one hundred twenty (120) days of the Committee's determination that documentation is complete.

    (c) Mandatory Proceedings¶

    No Supreme Court nominee shall be confirmed without:

    1. Public hearings before the Senate Judiciary Committee;

    2. Completion of background investigation by the Federal Bureau of Investigation;

    3. Public availability of all materials required under this Title for the periods specified herein.

    TITLE IV: IMPLEMENTATION AND FAIRNESS PROVISIONS¶

    Section 401. Implementation Timeline¶

    (a) Phase One: Circuit Reorganization¶

    • Administrative Office develops population-based circuit boundaries within 180 days of enactment

    • New circuit structure takes effect on the first January 1 occurring at least 24 months after enactment

    • Additional circuit judges appointed as needed

    (b) Phase Two: Gradual Court Expansion¶

    • Expansion begins: First odd-numbered year occurring at least 18 months after enactment

    • One justice per cycle: President nominates one additional Associate Justice each odd-numbered year

    • Fifteen Associate Justices achieved: When the sixth appointment under this Act occurs

    (c) Phase Three: Regular Replacement Schedule¶

    • Rotation begins: Twelve years after the first appointment under Phase Two

    • Career progression: Most senior Associate Justice becomes Chief Justice; after two years transitions to Senior Justice

    • Predictable appointments: Each presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities

    (d) Transparency Requirements¶

    All provisions of Title III apply immediately to any nomination submitted after the effective date of this Act.

    Section 402. Fairness and Balance Provisions¶

    (a) Bipartisan Implementation¶

    The expansion and replacement schedule ensures:

    1. No single president receives disproportionate appointment opportunities;

    2. Each four-year presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities;

    3. Regular turnover prevents excessive concentration of judicial power.

    (b) Constitutional Protections Preserved¶

    Nothing in this Act shall:

    1. Remove any sitting justice from office;

    2. Reduce the salary of any federal judge;

    3. Violate the "good behavior" tenure provision of Article III;

    4. Impair the independence of the federal judiciary.

    (c) Senior Justice Precedent¶

    The Senior Justice provisions build upon existing precedent in 28 U.S.C. Section 371 for senior status and maintain full Article III constitutional protections for all justices throughout their lifetime tenure.

    Section 403. Effective Date and Transition¶

    (a) Effective Date¶

    This Act shall take effect on January 1, 2026, except as otherwise provided.

    (b) Existing Justices¶

    Justices serving on the effective date:

    1. Are not subject to the three-phase career structure established by this Act;

    2. May continue in active service indefinitely or voluntarily elect Senior Justice status at any time after January 1, 2026;

    3. Maintain all constitutional protections and benefits regardless of service status.

    (c) Severability¶

    If any provision of this Act is held invalid, the remainder shall remain in effect.

    Section 404. Authorization of Appropriations¶

    There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary to carry out this Act, including:

    1. Additional judicial salaries and benefits;

    2. Court facilities and administrative costs;

    3. Implementation of circuit reorganization;

    4. Enhanced transparency and documentation review processes;

    5. Operations of the Office of the Judicial Inspector General and the Ethics Review Panel established under Title V.

    TITLE V: JUDICIAL ETHICS AND ACCOUNTABILITY¶

    Section 501. Adoption of the Code of Conduct¶

    (a) Application to the Supreme Court¶

    The "Code of Conduct for United States Judges," as adopted and amended by the Judicial Conference of the United States, shall apply in its entirety to the Justices of the Supreme Court of the United States.

    (b) Binding Effect¶

    Compliance with the Code of Conduct shall be mandatory, not advisory, for all Justices serving in active service or in Senior Justice status.

    (c) Amendments¶

    Any amendments to the Code of Conduct adopted by the Judicial Conference shall apply to Supreme Court Justices automatically upon adoption, unless Congress provides otherwise by statute.

    Section 502. Mandatory Recusal Documentation¶

    (a) Requirement¶

    Any Justice who recuses themselves from a proceeding, or who declines to recuse themselves following a formal motion by a party, shall provide a written explanation identifying the specific legal and factual basis for such decision.

    (b) Contents¶

    Such written explanation shall include:

    1. The specific provision of the Code of Conduct or federal statute implicated;

    2. The factual circumstances giving rise to the recusal question;

    3. The Justice's reasoning for the decision to recuse or not recuse.

    (c) Transparency¶

    Such explanations shall be:

    1. Made part of the public record of the proceeding;

    2. Published on the Supreme Court's official website within five business days of the decision;

    3. Compiled in an annual report to Congress.

    (d) Failure to Document¶

    Failure to provide written explanation as required by this section shall itself constitute a violation of the Code of Conduct subject to review under Section 505.

    Section 503. Establishment of the Ethics Review Panel¶

    (a) Composition¶

    There is established an Ethics Review Panel consisting of five Senior Justices selected as follows:

    1. The five Senior Justices who have most recently transitioned to Senior Justice status under Section 203 of this Act; or

    2. During the transition period described in Section 508, such Senior Justices as are available supplemented by members of the Bridge Panel established under Section 508(b).

    (b) Authority¶

    The Ethics Review Panel shall have the authority to:

    1. Receive reports and recommendations from the Judicial Inspector General established under Section 504;

    2. Adjudicate findings of ethical violations by Justices serving in active service;

    3. Impose or recommend sanctions from the menu established in Section 506;

    4. Recommend to the House of Representatives the initiation of impeachment proceedings in cases the Panel deems appropriate.

    (c) Independence¶

    1. No Justice serving in active service may participate in the deliberations of the Ethics Review Panel.

    2. No member of the Ethics Review Panel may participate in a matter in which they have a personal, financial, or familial interest, or involving conduct that occurred while they served alongside the respondent Justice in active service.

    3. Recusal of a Panel member shall be filled by the next most senior available Senior Justice, or during the transition period, by a member of the Bridge Panel.

    (d) Quorum and Voting¶

    1. Four members shall constitute a quorum for the transaction of business.

    2. Sanctions of private admonition or public censure may be imposed by a majority vote of the Panel.

    3. Sanctions of mandatory recusal, administrative reassignment, or mandatory early transition to Senior Justice status shall require the affirmative vote of at least four of the five Panel members.

    4. Recommendations of impeachment to the House of Representatives shall require the affirmative vote of at least four of the five Panel members.

    (e) Procedures¶

    The Ethics Review Panel shall adopt rules of procedure consistent with this Act, including procedures for:

    1. Receiving and reviewing reports from the Inspector General;

    2. Providing respondent Justices with notice and opportunity to be heard;

    3. Deliberation and decision-making;

    4. Issuance and publication of decisions.

    Section 504. Office of the Judicial Inspector General¶

    (a) Establishment¶

    There is established within the judicial branch the Office of the Judicial Inspector General, which shall be independent of the Supreme Court and all other federal courts.

    (b) Appointment¶

    1. The Judicial Inspector General shall be selected by the Ethics Review Panel from a list of three candidates submitted by a Nominating Committee.

    2. The Nominating Committee shall consist of:

    3. (A) The Comptroller General of the United States or their designee;
    4. (B) Two retired Chief Judges of United States Courts of Appeals, selected by lottery from among those who served at least ten years as a federal appellate judge; and

    5. (C) The President of the American Bar Association or their designee.

    6. The Nominating Committee shall submit only candidates who meet the qualifications specified in subsection (c).

    (c) Qualifications¶

    The Judicial Inspector General shall:

    1. Have served as an Article III judge for at least fifteen years, or have served in a position of equivalent legal distinction such as Solicitor General, Attorney General, or Dean of an accredited law school;

    2. Have no felony convictions;

    3. Not have held any position of leadership in a political party, political campaign, or political action committee within the ten years preceding nomination;

    4. Not be a close family member of any sitting Justice or any Justice who has served within the preceding ten years.

    (d) Term¶

    1. The Judicial Inspector General shall serve a single term of ten years and may not be reappointed.

    2. The term shall not be renewable to ensure independence from any appointing authority.

    (e) Removal¶

    1. The Judicial Inspector General may be removed only for inefficiency, neglect of duty, malfeasance in office, or incapacity.

    2. Removal shall require a unanimous vote of the Ethics Review Panel.

    3. Prior to any vote on removal, the Inspector General shall be afforded written notice of the grounds for removal and an opportunity to respond in writing and, if requested, in person before the Panel.

    (f) Duties¶

    The Judicial Inspector General shall:

    1. Receive complaints alleging violations of the Code of Conduct by Justices of the Supreme Court;

    2. Conduct preliminary review of complaints to determine whether they meet the threshold requirements of Section 505(b);

    3. Conduct investigations into complaints that survive preliminary review;

    4. Prepare reports of findings and submit them to the Ethics Review Panel;

    5. Maintain confidentiality of investigations as required by Section 505(e);

    6. Refer matters involving potential criminal conduct to the Department of Justice;

    7. Submit an annual report to the Ethics Review Panel and to Congress summarizing the Office's activities, without identifying the subjects of dismissed complaints or pending investigations.

    (g) Staff and Resources¶

    1. The Judicial Inspector General may appoint such staff as necessary to carry out the duties of the Office.

    2. All staff shall be subject to the same confidentiality requirements as the Inspector General.

    3. There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary to carry out this section.

    Section 505. Complaint Procedures and Investigation¶

    (a) Standing to File Complaints¶

    1. Any person may file a complaint with the Office of the Judicial Inspector General alleging that a Justice has violated the Code of Conduct.

    2. The House of Representatives or Senate, or the Judiciary Committee of either chamber, may file a complaint by formal resolution, which shall automatically proceed to investigation without preliminary review.

    3. The Ethics Review Panel may, by majority vote, direct the Inspector General to investigate a matter sua sponte.

    (b) Threshold Requirements¶

    1. The Inspector General shall summarily dismiss any complaint that:
    2. (A) Fails to identify a specific provision of the Code of Conduct allegedly violated;
    3. (B) Fails to allege facts with sufficient particularity to support a reasonable inference that a violation occurred;
    4. (C) Is based solely on the content of a Justice's judicial opinions, votes, or questions during oral argument;
    5. (D) Is duplicative of a complaint previously dismissed or adjudicated; or

    6. (E) Is facially frivolous or filed for purposes of harassment.

    7. Dismissal under this subsection shall be without prejudice to the filing of a new complaint that cures the deficiencies identified.

    (c) Preliminary Review¶

    1. For complaints not summarily dismissed under subsection (b), the Inspector General shall conduct a preliminary review to determine whether there is a reasonable basis to believe that a violation may have occurred.

    2. Preliminary review shall be completed within 60 days of receipt of the complaint.

    3. If preliminary review determines there is no reasonable basis to proceed, the Inspector General shall dismiss the complaint and provide a brief written explanation to the complainant.

    (d) Investigation¶

    1. If preliminary review determines there is a reasonable basis to believe a violation may have occurred, the Inspector General shall conduct an investigation.

    2. The Inspector General shall have authority to:

    3. (A) Review financial disclosure statements and other public records;
    4. (B) Request documents and information from the respondent Justice;
    5. (C) Interview witnesses;

    6. (D) Request documents and information from other federal agencies and courts.

    7. The respondent Justice shall cooperate with reasonable requests for information. Failure to cooperate may itself constitute a violation of the Code of Conduct.

    8. Upon completion of the investigation, the Inspector General shall prepare a written report containing findings of fact and submit it to the Ethics Review Panel.

    (e) Confidentiality¶

    1. All complaints, preliminary reviews, and investigations shall remain confidential until the Ethics Review Panel issues a final determination or the Panel votes to make findings public.

    2. The Inspector General, staff of the Office, and members of the Ethics Review Panel shall not disclose the existence or content of any complaint or investigation except as necessary to conduct the investigation or as authorized by this Act.

    3. Unauthorized disclosure by any person subject to this subsection shall constitute grounds for removal from office or termination of employment.

    4. Nothing in this subsection shall prevent a respondent Justice from publicly acknowledging that they are the subject of a complaint or investigation.

    (f) Due Process Protections¶

    Throughout all proceedings under this section, the respondent Justice shall be afforded:

    1. Written notice of the specific allegations and the provisions of the Code of Conduct allegedly violated;

    2. Access to all evidence gathered during the investigation, subject to reasonable protections for witness safety;

    3. The right to be represented by counsel of their choosing;

    4. The opportunity to submit a written response to the Inspector General's report;

    5. The opportunity to appear before the Ethics Review Panel, with counsel, before any sanction is imposed;

    6. A written decision from the Ethics Review Panel stating the findings and the basis for any sanction imposed.

    Section 506. Remedial Actions and Sanctions¶

    (a) Menu of Sanctions¶

    Upon a finding that a Justice has violated the Code of Conduct, the Ethics Review Panel may impose one or more of the following sanctions, as appropriate to the nature and severity of the conduct:

    1. Private Admonition: A confidential letter to the Justice identifying the violation and counseling against future similar conduct;

    2. Public Censure: A formal reprimand published in the United States Reports and on the Supreme Court's official website;

    3. Mandatory Recusal: A binding order prohibiting the Justice from participating in specified cases or categories of cases for a specified period;

    4. Administrative Reassignment: Removal of the Justice from circuit oversight duties, committee assignments, or other administrative responsibilities;

    5. Mandatory Early Transition: An order that the Justice shall transition to Senior Justice status under Section 203, effective upon exhaustion of the appellate review process established in Section 507;

    6. Referral for Impeachment: A formal report to the House Judiciary Committee documenting the Panel's findings and recommending consideration of impeachment proceedings.

    (b) Sanctions Requiring Supermajority¶

    The following sanctions may be imposed only upon the affirmative vote of at least four of the five members of the Ethics Review Panel:

    1. Mandatory Recusal;

    2. Administrative Reassignment;

    3. Mandatory Early Transition;

    4. Referral for Impeachment.

    (c) Enumerated Violations for Mandatory Early Transition¶

    The sanction of Mandatory Early Transition may be imposed only upon a finding, supported by clear and convincing evidence, that the Justice engaged in one or more of the following categories of conduct:

    1. Failure to disclose a financial interest exceeding $10,000 in a party or closely related entity in a matter before the Court, where such interest was required to be disclosed under applicable law or regulation;

    2. Participation in a matter in which the Justice had an undisclosed financial interest exceeding $50,000;

    3. Documented ex parte communication with a party, counsel, or agent of a party regarding the merits of a pending matter before the Court;

    4. Intentional material misrepresentation on financial disclosure forms or other mandatory filings;

    5. Acceptance of gifts, travel, or other benefits exceeding $5,000 in aggregate value in any calendar year from any person or entity that has appeared or is reasonably likely to appear before the Court, unless such gifts were properly disclosed and do not create an appearance of impropriety under circumstances where no reasonable jurist would accept them;

    6. A pattern of conduct demonstrating willful and persistent failure to comply with the Code of Conduct after prior sanction by the Panel.

    (d) Evidentiary Standards¶

    1. Sanctions of Private Admonition or Public Censure may be imposed upon a finding supported by a preponderance of the evidence.

    2. Sanctions of Mandatory Recusal or Administrative Reassignment may be imposed upon a finding supported by clear and convincing evidence.

    3. The sanction of Mandatory Early Transition may be imposed only upon a finding supported by clear and convincing evidence of conduct described in subsection (c).

    (e) Effect of Mandatory Early Transition¶

    1. A Justice subject to Mandatory Early Transition shall transition to Senior Justice status as defined in Section 203(c) of this Act.

    2. Such transition shall take effect upon:

    3. (A) The expiration of the period for seeking appellate review under Section 507 without a petition being filed; or

    4. (B) A final determination by the En Banc Senior Body upholding the Panel's decision.

    5. The Justice shall retain all attributes of Senior Justice status, including life tenure, full salary, and the ability to perform judicial duties as provided in Section 203(c).

    6. The vacancy created by Mandatory Early Transition shall be filled through the regular appointment process, with the President nominating a successor and the Senate providing advice and consent.

    (f) Referral for Impeachment¶

    1. Referral for Impeachment does not require a finding that the Justice committed a criminal offense.

    2. The Panel's report to the House Judiciary Committee shall include:

    3. (A) A complete statement of the findings of fact;
    4. (B) An analysis of how the conduct relates to the constitutional standard of "good Behaviour";

    5. (C) Any dissenting views of Panel members.

    6. Referral for Impeachment is not exclusive of other sanctions; the Panel may impose Mandatory Early Transition and refer for impeachment if it determines both are warranted.

    Section 507. Appellate Review by the En Banc Senior Body¶

    (a) Right to Petition¶

    Any Justice subject to a sanction of Mandatory Recusal, Administrative Reassignment, or Mandatory Early Transition may, within 30 days of the Ethics Review Panel's decision, petition for review by the full body of Senior Justices.

    (b) Mandatory Stay¶

    The filing of a petition for review shall automatically stay the enforcement of the sanction until the En Banc Senior Body has issued a final determination.

    (c) Composition¶

    1. The En Banc Senior Body shall consist of all Senior Justices except:
    2. (A) Any Senior Justice who served on the Ethics Review Panel for the matter under review; and

    3. (B) Any Senior Justice who has a conflict of interest requiring recusal under standards applicable to the Panel.

    4. During the transition period described in Section 508, the En Banc Senior Body shall include all available Senior Justices and members of the Bridge Panel who did not participate in the Panel decision under review.

    5. A quorum for the En Banc Senior Body shall be the greater of:

    6. (A) Five members; or
    7. (B) A majority of the eligible members.

    (d) Standard of Review¶

    1. The En Banc Senior Body shall review the Ethics Review Panel's findings of fact for clear error.

    2. The En Banc Senior Body shall review the Panel's choice of sanction for abuse of discretion.

    3. The En Banc Senior Body may affirm, reverse, or modify the Panel's decision, or remand for further proceedings.

    (e) Decision¶

    1. The En Banc Senior Body shall issue a written decision within 90 days of the filing of the petition.

    2. The decision shall include a statement of reasons.

    3. Dissenting members may file separate opinions.

    (f) Finality¶

    1. The decision of the En Banc Senior Body shall be the final administrative determination of the Judicial Branch.

    2. Nothing in this section shall limit the constitutional authority of the House of Representatives to initiate impeachment proceedings or the authority of the full Congress to conduct oversight.

    Section 508. Transition Provisions for Title V¶

    (a) Transition Period Defined¶

    For purposes of this Title, the "transition period" begins on the effective date of this Act and ends when at least five Justices appointed under the expansion schedule established in Section 201(c) have transitioned to Senior Justice status.

    (b) Bridge Panel¶

    1. During the transition period, if fewer than five Senior Justices are available to serve on the Ethics Review Panel, the remaining positions shall be filled by members of a Bridge Panel.

    2. The Bridge Panel shall consist of retired Chief Judges of United States Courts of Appeals selected as follows:

    3. (A) The Administrative Office of the United States Courts shall maintain a roster of all living former Chief Judges of the Courts of Appeals who served at least fifteen years as an Article III judge;
    4. (B) Bridge Panel members shall be selected by lottery from this roster as needed to fill vacancies on the Ethics Review Panel;

    5. (C) Selection by lottery shall be conducted publicly by the Clerk of the Supreme Court.

    6. Bridge Panel members shall serve until:

    7. (A) A Senior Justice becomes available to fill the position; or

    8. (B) The matter or matters for which they were selected are fully resolved, including any appellate review.

    9. Bridge Panel members shall have all the powers and responsibilities of Senior Justices for purposes of this Title, except that they may not participate in matters arising under other Titles of this Act.

    (c) Voluntary Senior Status During Transition¶

    1. Justices serving on the effective date of this Act who voluntarily elect Senior Justice status under Section 403(b) may serve on the Ethics Review Panel.

    2. No Justice who voluntarily elects Senior Justice status during the transition period may participate in a matter involving conduct that occurred while they served alongside the respondent Justice in active service.

    (d) Initial Implementation¶

    1. The first Judicial Inspector General shall be appointed within one year of the effective date of this Act.

    2. Until the Inspector General is appointed, the existing Office of Inspector General for the Administrative Office of the United States Courts may receive complaints, but shall not conduct investigations or preliminary reviews under this Title.

    3. The Ethics Review Panel shall adopt initial rules of procedure within 180 days of first achieving a quorum.

    Section 509. Preservation of Constitutional Authorities¶

    (a) Impeachment¶

    Nothing in this Title shall be construed to limit, modify, or supersede the constitutional authority of the House of Representatives to impeach or of the Senate to try impeachments.

    (b) Judicial Review¶

    The procedures and sanctions established by this Title are internal to the Judicial Branch and shall not be subject to review by any court, except that:

    1. A Justice subject to Mandatory Early Transition may seek review from the En Banc Senior Body as provided in Section 507; and

    2. Questions regarding the constitutionality of this Title may be raised in appropriate proceedings.

    (c) Criminal Liability¶

    Nothing in this Title shall be construed to immunize any person from criminal liability under federal law, nor to limit the authority of the Department of Justice to investigate or prosecute criminal conduct.
