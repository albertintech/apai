    FEDERAL JUDICIAL BALANCE AND ACCOUNTABILITY ACT¶

    A Bill to Modernize the Federal Judiciary, Establish Population-Based Judicial Circuits, Expand the Supreme Court, and Ensure Balanced Representation¶

    Revision 1.2¶

    SECTION 1: SHORT TITLE¶

    This Act may be cited as the "Federal Judicial Balance and Accountability Act."

    SECTION 2: FINDINGS AND PURPOSES¶

    (a) Findings¶

    Congress finds that:

    1. Article III of the Constitution vests judicial power in "one supreme Court, and in such inferior Courts as the Congress may from time to time ordain and establish";

    2. The Constitution provides that judges "shall hold their Offices during good Behaviour" and receive compensation that "shall not be diminished during their Continuance in Office";

    3. Congress has constitutional authority under Article III and the Necessary and Proper Clause to organize and structure the federal judiciary to ensure efficient administration of justice;

    4. The current federal circuit system reflects historical boundaries rather than population distribution, creating unequal representation and workload disparities;

    5. The Supreme Court has maintained nine justices since 1869, despite the creation of additional federal circuits and dramatic population growth;

    6. The constitutional tradition of Supreme Court justices serving as circuit justices creates a direct representational relationship between regional circuits and the Supreme Court, with optimal judicial administration achieved when each regional circuit is served by one Supreme Court justice;

    7. Regular and predictable judicial appointments promote constitutional balance and reduce partisan manipulation of the appointment process;

    8. Clear definition of judicial duties and responsibilities promotes effective court administration and public confidence in the judiciary;

    9. Transparent qualification standards enhance the legitimacy and quality of judicial appointments;

    10. The current system creates arbitrary advantages for presidents based on actuarial chance rather than constitutional design;

    11. The position of Chief Justice should rotate among justices as a capstone to distinguished service rather than concentrating administrative power in a single individual for decades.

    (b) Purposes¶

    The purposes of this Act are to:

    1. Establish population-based federal judicial circuits to ensure equal representation;

    2. Align Supreme Court composition with regional circuit organization to restore constitutional balance between Court size and judicial administration;

    3. Create predictable and fair judicial appointment processes;

    4. Define the duties and responsibilities of federal judicial offices;

    5. Establish minimum qualifications for judicial service;

    6. Enhance transparency in the judicial appointment process;

    7. Ensure optimal utilization of judicial experience and expertise;

    8. Restore constitutional balance among the branches of government;

    9. Diffuse the concentration of power in the Chief Justice position through automatic rotation.

    SECTION 3: DEFINITIONS¶

    In this Act:

    1. "Active service" means the performance of regular duties as a member of the court to which appointed;

    2. "Senior service" means judicial service in a capacity other than active service on the court of original appointment;

    3. "Supreme Court justice" means a person holding the office of Chief Justice or Associate Justice of the Supreme Court of the United States;

    4. "Federal judge" means any judge appointed under Article III of the Constitution;

    5. "Population-based circuit" means a judicial circuit organized primarily according to population distribution rather than state boundaries;

    6. "Regional circuit" means one of the fifteen numbered judicial circuits serving approximately equal populations across the United States.

    TITLE I: FEDERAL CIRCUIT REORGANIZATION¶

    Section 101. Establishment of Fifteen Judicial Circuits¶

    (a) Circuit Structure¶

    Beginning January 1, 2027, the United States shall be divided into fifteen judicial circuits for the administration of federal justice, organized as follows:

    1. Fifteen Regional Circuits numbered First through Fifteenth, each serving approximately equal populations as determined by the most recent decennial census;

    2. District of Columbia Circuit retaining specialized jurisdiction over federal regulatory, administrative, and governmental matters;

    3. Federal Circuit retaining specialized jurisdiction over patents, international trade, federal employment, and appeals from specialized federal courts.

    (b) Population-Based Boundaries¶

    The Administrative Office of the United States Courts shall, within 180 days of enactment:

    1. Divide the national population (excluding territories) into fifteen approximately equal segments;

    2. Assign each segment to a numbered circuit, prioritizing geographic contiguity where feasible;

    3. Ensure no single circuit serves more than 110% or less than 90% of the average circuit population.

    (c) Decennial Rebalancing¶

    Following each decennial census, the Administrative Office shall:

    1. Recalculate optimal circuit boundaries to maintain population equality;

    2. Propose boundary adjustments to Congress;

    3. Implement approved adjustments effective January 1 following the census.

    (d) Specialized Circuits Unchanged¶

    The District of Columbia Circuit and Federal Circuit shall retain their current jurisdictions and organizational structures.

    Section 102. Judicial Appointments to New Circuits¶

    (a) Circuit Judges¶

    Within two years of circuit reorganization:

    1. The President shall nominate, and the Senate shall consider, sufficient circuit judges to maintain adequate judicial resources in each circuit;

    2. Each regional circuit shall have no fewer than six active judges;

    3. Existing circuit judges shall be reassigned according to the jurisdiction where they maintain primary residence.

    (b) District Judges¶

    District court structure and judgeships shall continue unchanged, except that:

    1. District courts shall be assigned to new circuit jurisdictions based on geographic location;

    2. Appeals from district courts shall proceed to the appropriate regional circuit;

    3. Congress may create additional district judgeships as needed.

    TITLE II: SUPREME COURT COMPOSITION AND APPOINTMENTS¶

    Section 201. Supreme Court Expansion¶

    (a) Fifteen Justice Supreme Court¶

    The Supreme Court of the United States shall consist of fifteen Associate Justices and one Chief Justice, for a total of sixteen members.

    (b) Alignment with Circuits¶

    The Supreme Court composition reflects:

    1. Fifteen regional judicial circuits, each represented by one Associate Justice;

    2. One Chief Justice serving administrative and ceremonial functions;

    3. Restoration of the constitutional relationship between circuit organization and Supreme Court size.

    (c) Phased Expansion Schedule¶

    The Court shall expand gradually according to the following timeline:

    1. Initial Expansion (Years 1-6): One additional justice appointed in each odd-numbered year until fifteen Associate Justice positions exist;

    2. Regular Replacement (Year 13+): Beginning twelve years after the first appointment under this Act, one justice transitions to senior service each odd-numbered year;

    3. Steady State: Once fully implemented, each presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities.

    Section 202. Appointment Process¶

    (a) Presidential Nomination¶

    The President shall nominate individuals to fill Supreme Court vacancies within 90 days of a vacancy occurring or being scheduled.

    (b) Senate Consideration¶

    The Senate shall:

    1. Hold confirmation hearings within 60 days of receiving a nomination;

    2. Vote on confirmation within 120 days of receiving a nomination;

    3. Provide a floor vote unless the nominee withdraws or the President rescinds the nomination.

    (c) Predictable Appointment Schedule¶

    Beginning twelve years after the first appointment under Section 201(c):

    1. One Associate Justice position becomes available each odd-numbered year;

    2. The most senior justice appointed under this Act's expansion schedule transitions to senior service;

    3. Each four-year presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities.

    Section 203. Senior Service for Justices¶

    (a) Transition to Senior Service¶

    After twelve years of active service on the Supreme Court, a justice transitions to senior service, where they may:

    1. Continue to hear and decide cases as assigned by the Chief Justice;

    2. Sit by designation on federal courts of appeals;

    3. Serve on judicial panels, commissions, and committees;

    4. Participate in oral arguments and deliberations when assigned;

    5. Decline assignments without forfeiting judicial status or compensation.

    (b) Constitutional Protections Maintained¶

    Justices in senior service:

    1. Retain their Article III status and constitutional protections;

    2. Continue to receive full judicial salary and benefits;

    3. Maintain life tenure during "good behavior";

    4. Exercise full judicial powers when assigned to cases.

    (c) Voluntary Early Transition¶

    Any justice may elect senior service before completing twelve years of active service, creating an immediate vacancy for presidential appointment.

    Section 204. Chief Justice¶

    (a) Automatic Selection¶

    Beginning when the first justice appointed under Section 201(c) reaches their eleventh year of active service:

    1. The Associate Justice with the longest tenure in active service who has not previously served as Chief Justice shall become Chief Justice upon commencing their eleventh year of active service;

    2. No separate nomination or confirmation shall be required;

    3. The transition to Chief Justice shall occur automatically on the anniversary of the justice's appointment to active service.

    (b) Term¶

    The Chief Justice serves for two years, comprising their eleventh and twelfth years of active service, after which they transition directly to senior service under Section 203.

    (c) Transition Period¶

    Until the automatic selection process begins under subsection (a):

    1. The Chief Justice serving on the effective date of this Act shall continue in that capacity until death, voluntary election of senior service, or removal;

    2. If a vacancy in the Chief Justice position occurs before the automatic selection process begins, the President shall designate a Chief Justice from among the sitting Associate Justices, subject to Senate confirmation, to serve until the automatic selection process begins.

    (d) Duties and Powers¶

    The Chief Justice shall:

    1. Preside over Court sessions and conferences;

    2. Assign majority opinions when in the majority;

    3. Serve as administrative head of the federal judiciary;

    4. Preside over presidential impeachment trials in the Senate;

    5. Exercise no greater voting power than Associate Justices.

    (e) Incapacity or Vacancy¶

    1. If the Chief Justice is temporarily unable to perform duties, the Associate Justice with the longest tenure in active service shall serve as Acting Chief Justice;

    2. If the Chief Justice dies, is removed, or elects early senior service during their term, the Associate Justice next in line under the automatic selection process shall become Chief Justice for the remainder of their own eleventh and twelfth years of active service;

    3. If no Associate Justice has reached their eleventh year of active service when a vacancy occurs, subsection (c)(2) shall apply.

    TITLE III: QUALIFICATIONS AND TRANSPARENCY¶

    Section 301. Minimum Qualifications for Supreme Court Justices¶

    (a) Professional Requirements¶

    No person shall be appointed to the Supreme Court unless they:

    1. Is a citizen of the United States and has been such for at least 10 years;

    2. Has received a juris doctor degree from a law school accredited by the American Bar Association;

    3. Has been admitted to practice law before the highest court of a State and is in good standing;

    4. Has at least 15 years of legal experience, including:

    5. (A) practice of law;
    6. (B) service as a judge; or

    7. (C) teaching of law at an accredited institution;

    8. Has served as a federal appellate judge for at least 5 years; and

    9. Has no felony convictions.

    (b) Enhanced Qualifications Review¶

    Each nominee shall undergo comprehensive review including:

    1. Character Assessment: Full FBI background investigation and ethics review;

    2. Professional Competence: Review of all judicial opinions, legal writings, and professional conduct;

    3. Financial Disclosure: Complete financial transparency and conflict of interest analysis.

    Section 302. Transparency Requirements¶

    (a) Mandatory Documentation¶

    Each Supreme Court nominee shall provide:

    1. Professional Biography: Detailed account of legal career, including:
    2. All employment history, including non-legal positions
    3. Types of legal work performed and clients served
    4. Community service and volunteer activities

    5. Leadership roles and professional achievements

    6. Personal Background Statement: Written account of:

    7. Educational background and academic achievements
    8. Personal challenges overcome and life experiences
    9. Geographic and cultural diversity of experiences

    10. Economic background and understanding of diverse circumstances

    11. Judicial Philosophy Statement: Comprehensive explanation of:

    12. Approach to constitutional interpretation
    13. View of judicial restraint versus judicial activism
    14. Role of precedent in legal decision-making
    15. Specific areas of legal expertise and interest

    (b) Public Disclosure¶

    All documentation required under subsection (a) shall be made publicly available at least 30 days before Senate confirmation hearings.

    Section 303. Appointment Process Reforms¶

    (a) Consideration Timeline¶

    The Senate shall complete its consideration of a Supreme Court nominee within 120 days of receiving the nomination, unless extended by majority vote for extraordinary circumstances.

    (b) Mandatory Proceedings¶

    No Supreme Court nominee shall be confirmed without:

    1. Public hearings before the Senate Judiciary Committee;

    2. Completion of FBI background investigation;

    3. Review by a judicial qualifications panel consisting of:

    4. Two federal judges selected by the Chief Justice;
    5. Two practicing attorneys selected by the American Bar Association;
    6. One law professor selected by the Association of American Law Schools.

    (c) Ethics Review¶

    Prior to confirmation, each nominee shall undergo ethics review addressing:

    1. Financial disclosures and potential conflicts of interest;

    2. Compliance with applicable codes of judicial conduct;

    3. Any pending investigations or unresolved ethical concerns.

    TITLE IV: IMPLEMENTATION AND FAIRNESS PROVISIONS¶

    Section 401. Implementation Timeline¶

    (a) Phase One: Circuit Reorganization¶

    • Administrative Office develops population-based circuit boundaries within 180 days of enactment

    • New circuit structure takes effect on the first January 1 occurring at least 24 months after enactment

    • Additional circuit judges appointed as needed

    (b) Phase Two: Gradual Court Expansion¶

    • Expansion begins: First odd-numbered year occurring at least 18 months after enactment

    • One justice per cycle: President nominates one additional justice each odd-numbered year

    • Fifteen justices achieved: When the sixth appointment under this Act occurs

    (c) Phase Three: Regular Replacement Schedule¶

    • Rotation begins: Twelve years after the first appointment under Phase Two

    • One justice transition: Most senior justice appointed under this Act transitions to senior service each odd-numbered year

    • Predictable appointments: Each presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities

    (d) Qualification Requirements¶

    All provisions of Title III apply immediately to any justice nominated after the effective date of this Act.

    Section 402. Fairness and Balance Provisions¶

    (a) Bipartisan Implementation¶

    The expansion and replacement schedule ensures:

    1. No single president receives disproportionate appointment opportunities;

    2. Each four-year presidential term includes exactly two appointment opportunities;

    3. Regular turnover prevents excessive concentration of judicial power.

    (b) Constitutional Protections Preserved¶

    Nothing in this Act shall:

    1. Remove any sitting justice from office;

    2. Reduce the salary of any federal judge;

    3. Violate the "good behavior" tenure provision of Article III;

    4. Impair the independence of the federal judiciary.

    (c) Senior Status Precedent¶

    The senior service provisions build upon existing precedent in 28 U.S.C. Section 371 and maintain full Article III constitutional protections for all justices.

    Section 403. Effective Date and Transition¶

    (a) Effective Date¶

    This Act shall take effect on January 1, 2026, except as otherwise provided.

    (b) Existing Justices¶

    Justices serving on the effective date may:

    1. Continue in active service until designated for transition under the regular schedule;

    2. Voluntarily elect senior service at any time after January 1, 2026;

    3. Maintain all constitutional protections and benefits regardless of service status.

    (c) Severability¶

    If any provision of this Act is held invalid, the remainder shall remain in effect.

    Section 404. Authorization of Appropriations¶

    There are authorized to be appropriated such sums as may be necessary to carry out this Act, including:

    1. Additional judicial salaries and benefits;

    2. Court facilities and administrative costs;

    3. Implementation of circuit reorganization;

    4. Enhanced qualification review processes.

    Revision History¶

    Revision 1.2 (Current)

    • Revised Section 204 to establish automatic Chief Justice rotation
    • Chief Justice now selected automatically by seniority for final two years of active service (years 11-12)
    • Eliminates separate nomination and confirmation for Chief Justice position
    • Every justice who completes full term serves as Chief Justice
    • Added transition period provisions for period before automatic rotation begins
    • Added incapacity and vacancy provisions for Chief Justice position
    • Added findings (11) and purposes (9) regarding Chief Justice rotation and power diffusion
